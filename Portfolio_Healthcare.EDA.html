Exploratory Data Analysis - Statistical Testing

Now we have a pretty good idea of what the dataset look like lets see if we could answer some interesting questions based on this dataset.

Patient Demographics and Medical Conditions¶

What is the relationship between age and medical conditions?¶

Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) is a statistical method used to compare the means of three or more groups to determine if there is a statistically significant difference between them. It tests the hypothesis that the means of different groups are equal by analyzing the variance within each group and the variance between the groups.

How ANOVA Answers Our Research Question¶

Our research question aims to determine if there is a significant relationship between age and medical conditions. Specifically, we want to know if the mean age of patients differs across various medical conditions.

Why ANOVA is Appropriate:

  • Comparing Multiple Groups: We have multiple medical conditions, each representing a group. ANOVA allows us to compare the mean ages across these groups simultaneously.
  • Statistical Significance: ANOVA provides a p-value to determine if any observed differences in means are statistically significant. A low p-value (typically < 0.05) indicates that at least one group mean is significantly different from the others.
  • Variance Analysis: By analyzing the variance within each medical condition group and the variance between the groups, ANOVA helps us understand if age differences are more pronounced within groups or between them.

Interpretation¶

  • Significant Difference: If the p-value from the ANOVA test is less than 0.05, it indicates that there is a statistically significant difference in the mean ages across different medical conditions. This means that age is related to the type of medical condition.
  • No Significant Difference: If the p-value is greater than 0.05, it suggests that there is no significant difference in the mean ages, implying that age does not vary significantly across different medical conditions.

By using ANOVA, we can rigorously test our hypothesis and draw meaningful conclusions about the relationship between age and medical conditions, which can inform healthcare strategies and interventions.

Summary¶

The p-value of 0.669432 indicates that there is no statistically significant difference in the mean age across different medical conditions. Therefore, we conclude that medical conditions do not significantly affect the patient age in this dataset.

Is there a significant difference in the distribution of medical conditions between genders?¶

Description of Pearson Chi-Square Test

The Pearson Chi-Square Test is a statistical test used to determine if there is a significant association between two categorical variables. It compares the observed frequencies of occurrences in each category of a contingency table with the frequencies that would be expected if there were no association between the variables. The test statistic measures how far the observed frequencies deviate from the expected frequencies, and the p-value indicates whether this deviation is statistically significant.

How the Pearson Chi-Square Test Applies to Our Research Question

Our research question aims to determine if there is a significant difference in the distribution of medical conditions between genders. Specifically, we want to know if the occurrence of different medical conditions is associated with the patient's gender. Since both gender and medical conditions are categorical variables, the Pearson Chi-Square Test is appropriate for this analysis.

Why Pearson Chi-Square Test is Appropriate:

  • Categorical Data: Both 'Gender' and 'Medical Condition' are categorical variables, making the Chi-Square Test suitable for assessing the association between them.
  • Contingency Table Analysis: The test analyzes the frequencies in a contingency table, comparing the observed counts of each medical condition across genders to the counts expected if there were no association.
  • Statistical Significance: The Chi-Square Test provides a p-value to determine if any observed differences in frequencies are statistically significant. A low p-value (typically < 0.05) indicates a significant association between the variables.

Interpretation:

  • Significant Association: If the p-value from the Chi-Square Test is less than 0.05, it indicates a significant association between gender and medical condition. This means that the distribution of medical conditions varies significantly between males and females.
  • No Significant Association: If the p-value is greater than 0.05, it suggests no significant association between gender and medical condition, implying that the distribution of medical conditions is similar across genders.

By using the Pearson Chi-Square Test, we can rigorously assess whether gender influences the distribution of medical conditions in our dataset, providing valuable insights for healthcare strategies and interventions.

Analysis of Billing Amount Variation with Age and Duration of Stay Using Linear Regression¶

Linear regression is a statistical method used to model the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables. In this context, we are examining how the billing amount (dependent variable) varies with age and the duration of stay (independent variables). By fitting a linear regression model, we aim to determine if there is a significant relationship between these factors and the billing amount.

What is Regression?¶

Regression analysis helps in understanding the relationship between variables. In linear regression, the goal is to fit a linear equation to observed data. The equation of the form:

$$ \text{Billing Amount} = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot \text{Age} + \beta_2 \cdot \text{Duration of Stay} + \epsilon $$

where:

  • $\beta_0$ is the intercept,
  • $\beta_1$ and (\beta_2) are the coefficients for age and duration of stay respectively,
  • $\epsilon$ is the error term.

Why Does Regression Answer Our Research Question?¶

  1. Identify Relationship: Regression allows us to identify whether there is a statistically significant relationship between age, duration of stay, and the billing amount.
  2. Quantify Effect: It quantifies the effect of age and duration of stay on billing amount, providing a measure of how much billing amounts change with each unit increase in age or duration of stay.
  3. Predict Future Values: The regression model can be used to predict future billing amounts based on age and duration of stay, aiding in planning and decision-making.
  4. Assess Significance: By evaluating the significance of the coefficients, we can determine if the observed relationships are not due to random chance, thus giving us confidence in the findings.

Overall, linear regression is a powerful tool for exploring and understanding the factors that influence billing amounts, making it an appropriate method for addressing our research question.
