The Nowegian Salmon price shows a trend of increasing price and monthly flutuation. We can isolate the random fluation forom predictable change by performing differencing on the Salmon price. By taking the diffence between pairs of lag 1 we can eliminate the increasing trend and differencing lag 12 will eliminate the seasonal trend.

In [3]:
options(repr.plot.width = 15, repr.plot.height = 10)
plot.ts(cbind(salmon,diff(salmon,1),diff(diff(salmon,1),12)  ), main = "Price Vs. Time", ylab = "export price")

The plots appear to become more random as we remove the predictable underlying trend. This gives us some idea of which model to employ. It seems SARIMA is a good model that also accounts for seasonality. The differenced time series could be fitted with an ARMA model first. We use the common technique of observing the autocorrelation function(ACF) and partial auto-correlation function(PACF) of the processed series.

The fitted model is SARIMA(1,1,0)(0,0,1)[12], a seasonal ARIMA model that can be described by the equation: $$ (1 - \psi_1 B) (X_t - X_{t-1}) = (1 + \Theta_1 B^{12}) w_t $$ where $X_t$ denote the time series value at time $t$, $B$ satify $B^k X_t = X_{t-k} $ and $w_t$ a white noise series. The estimated values of the model parameters are: $$ \hat{\psi}_1 = 0.2796 \quad and \quad \hat{\Theta}_1 = 0.2139 $$

Next we will be performing model diagonostic of the fitted SARIMA model. We are testing weather the fitted model statisfies the assumptions the residual should be a white noise distribution.

The diagnostic tells us that the residual is not independently distributed and they exhibit serial correlation. But it looks like we are close, the ACF still displays serial correlation at a period of 12 that an indication that the seasonal component of the model needs updating. Lets try a SARIMA(1,1,0)(0,1,1)[12] using a further differencing of a period of 12 to account for the periodicity in the ACF plot.
